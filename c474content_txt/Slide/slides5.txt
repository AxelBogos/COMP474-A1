2006\nhttps://www.w3.org/DesignIssues/LinkedData.html\n\nWhat\xe2\x80\x99s a \xe2\x80\x9clink\xe2\x80\x9d?\nTechnically, a link is a triple where\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2 the subject IRI is in one graph (namespace) and\n\xe2\x80\xa2 the predicate and/or object are in a different graph (namespace)\n\nTypes of Links\n\nIdentity Link: points to the same object or concept in another knowledge graph\n(e.g., Canada in DBpedia and Wikidata)\n\nRelationship Link: points to other objects related to a given one (e.g., from a person\nto the city they live in)\n\nVocabulary Link: between data and their vocabulary term\n(e.g., IRI is a foaf:Person) or between vocabulary terms\n(e.g., ex:Student rdfs:subClassOf foaf:Person)\n\nhttps://www.w3.org/DesignIssues/LinkedData.html\n\n\nRen\xc3\xa9 (IRIs):\nowl:sameAs Most widely used, but makes strong assumptions about both IRIs\n\nbeing identical (it is symmetric and transitive)\nrdfs:seeAlso Just a reference, makes no claim about the IRIs being identical\n\n(no specific semantics)\nusing vocabularies e.g., skos:exactMatch, foaf:knows\n\nSchema Level\nTwo graphs can also be linked on the schema level:\n\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Using owl:equivalentClass and owl:equivalentProperty\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Using rdfs:subClassOf and rdfs:subPropertyOf\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Using vocabularies, e.g., skos:broadMatch or skos:narrowMatch\n\n\n\nRen\xc3\xa9
